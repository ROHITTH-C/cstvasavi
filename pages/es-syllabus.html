Environmental Science - Syllabus

UNIT-I: Multidisciplinary Nature of Environmental Studies

Definition, Scope and Importance – Need for Public Awareness. Natural Resources: Renewable and non-renewable resources – Problems related to natural resources – Forest resources: Use and over-exploitation, deforestation, case studies – Water resources: Utilization, conflicts, and management – Mineral resources: Extraction and environmental effects – Food resources: World food problems, agriculture impact – Energy resources.

UNIT-II: Ecosystems & Biodiversity Conservation

Concept, structure, and function of ecosystems – Energy flow – Ecological succession – Food chains, food webs, and pyramids. Types of ecosystems: Forest, Grassland, Desert, and Aquatic ecosystems. Biodiversity: Genetic, species, and ecosystem diversity – Biogeographical classification of India – Values and threats to biodiversity – Conservation methods.

UNIT-III: Environmental Pollution

Definition, causes, effects, and control of air, water, soil, marine, noise, thermal, and nuclear pollution. Solid Waste Management: Urban and industrial waste control – Role of individuals in pollution prevention – Disaster management: Floods, earthquakes, cyclones, landslides.

UNIT-IV: Social Issues and Environment

Sustainable development – Urban energy issues – Water conservation – Resettlement and rehabilitation concerns – Environmental ethics – Climate change impacts – Environmental protection laws – Public awareness.

UNIT-V: Human Population and the Environment

Population growth and variation – Family welfare programs – Human health and environment – Human rights – Value education – HIV/AIDS awareness – Role of IT in environmental protection. Field Work: Site visits to study environmental assets, pollution, and biodiversity.
